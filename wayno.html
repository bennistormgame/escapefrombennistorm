Wayno is the director behind WJC · Wayne J C Gaming — and the human at the centre of Wibbles & Wobbles. He didn't come from games. He didn't write the code. What he had was a clear vision, stubborn taste, and a refusal to let "I can't build that myself" be the end of the idea.

So he did the next best thing: he assembled a crew. Not of people — of AI specialists, each with a job, each pointed at the same goal. He directed. They built. The result is a full, polished game with 100 levels, a 34-strong cast and a cinematic story — made by one person who never opened a code editor.

// how it happened

From a blank screen to Bennistorm.

The short version of a long, stubborn climb. (Outline for now — the full story gets fleshed out as we go.)

01

THE IDEA

A world, a cast of misfits and one impossible night out. No studio, no budget, no code — just a premise too good to leave in a notebook.

02

ASSEMBLE THE CREW

Instead of hiring a team, Wayno built one out of AI — a strategist, a builder, a writer, a critic, a release engineer and a brand lead. Each given a name, a remit and a standard to hit.

03

BUILD THE GAME

Level by level, character by character. 100 levels, 5 worlds, an arcade hidden inside the arcade, an original soundtrack and a full cinematic episode — directed, reviewed, polished, repeat.

04

SHIP IT

Onto real phones, then toward Android and Steam — and this very site, built by the crew too. Episode One is the proof. It is not the end of the plan.
